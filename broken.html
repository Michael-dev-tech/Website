Microsoft: "30% of Our Code is AI." Also Microsoft: "Windows is Broken."

May 2, 2025

You can't make this stuff up. In the same week, we get two headlines that tell you everything you need to know about the current state of modern software.

First, Satya Nadella proudly announces that 30% of Microsoft's code is now written by AI. He spins it as "velocity" and "innovation." A few hours later? Microsoft admits that "almost all major Windows 11 core features are broken."

Connect the dots. They fired the humans, let a hallucinating chatbot write the kernel, and then shipped the beta to us. This isn't a bug; it's the new business model. They are degrading the product to save money on developers, and we are paying the price in stability and privacy.

Windows has officially become a Frankenstein's monster of AI-generated spaghetti code. If 30% of the OS is written by a machine that doesn't understand context, it's no wonder the Start Menu doesn't work. Get off this sinking ship.
