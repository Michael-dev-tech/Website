A Little Update: 7-Zip Just Got a Big Boost!

Jul 05, 2025

Hey everyone,

Just wanted to share some quick news that caught my eye. If you're anything like me, 7-Zip is probably one of those unsung heroes living quietly in your utility belt. It's just *there*, doing its job, usually without much fanfare. But version 25.00 just dropped, and honestly, it's a pretty solid upgrade that might make your day a bit snappier, especially if you're dealing with a lot of files.

So, What's New? The Good Stuff:

The big headlines here are all about speed and efficiency, and they're definitely worth talking about:

  • Serious Multi-Core Muscle: This is a game-changer for anyone with a beefy CPU. 7-Zip on Windows can now really flex its muscles, using over 64 CPU threads for compression. If your machine has multiple processor groups (which is common on higher-end systems), 7-Zip intelligently spreads the workload, meaning you're finally getting the absolute most out of all those cores when you're zipping up huge archives. Think faster packing of those massive projects!
  • Compression Gets a Kick:
    • We're talking a **15-40% speed jump for bzip2 compression**. That's a huge improvement if you rely on that format.
    • Even the common **deflate (ZIP/GZ) compression got a nice 1-3% bump**. It might sound small, but over hundreds of files, those seconds add up!
  • Smoother Handling for More Formats: They've also polished up how 7-Zip deals with various archive types â€“ specifically ZIP, CPIO, and FAT. This means fewer headaches and more reliable unpacking no matter where your files came from.
  • Under-the-Hood Polish: Beyond the big features, they've also squashed a few bugs and tightened up security. Always good to know your tools are getting safer and more stable.

Honestly, it's the kind of incremental improvement that you don't always notice until you really need it. But when you're compressing a huge chunk of data, every bit of speed helps. If you're using an older version of 7-Zip, now might be a great time to grab this update. It's free, and it just makes a great tool even better.
