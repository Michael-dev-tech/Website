A Little Update: 7-Zip Just Got a Big Boost!

Jul 05, 2025

Hey everyone,

I was fiddling with my file archives the other day and noticed something interesting: 7-Zip version 25.00 is out now, and it’s packed with tweaks that actually make a difference. If you’ve ever sat impatiently watching a large folder compress, this release might ease some of that waiting.

Embracing All Those CPU Cores

If your PC boasts a multi-core processor, you’re in luck. 7-Zip on Windows can now tap into more than 64 threads for compression work. That means it spreads the load across every available core and processor group, shaving off precious seconds when you’re packaging huge projects.

Noticeable Speed Gains

For anyone relying on bzip2, expect a 15–40% jump in compression speed—pretty dramatic if you ask me. Even the familiar ZIP and GZ formats see a small but welcome 1–3% boost. It may not sound like much at first, but when you’re running batches of files, every bit counts.

Smoother Archive Handling

Aside from speed, the update also refines how 7-Zip interacts with different archive types. ZIP, CPIO, and FAT formats have received under-the-hood improvements, which translates to fewer hiccups when extracting files created on other systems.

Bits and Bobs Under the Hood

Beyond the headline features, the developer team has ironed out a handful of bugs and tightened security in the codebase. It’s the kind of maintenance update you barely notice—until something that used to crash suddenly works flawlessly.

If you haven’t updated in a while, now’s a great time to download the latest 7-Zip. It’s free, it plays nicely with all your files, and suddenly, those compression tasks feel a bit more lightweight.
